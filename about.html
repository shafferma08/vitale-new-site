About Us

Our Story

How a sketch on a napkin in 1992 became St. Pete's most iconic mural studio.

EST. 1992

Vitale Bros. has been a fixture in the Tampa Bay art scene since 1992. Based in St. Petersburg, FL, the studio has painted walls throughout the region and played a major role in making St. Petersburg a world-class arts destination.

It all started with hustle. Johnny Vitale got his start hand lettering signs, painting storefront windows, and eventually landed his first mural after approaching a restaurant owner with a sketch on a paper napkin. That scrappy, grassroots approach defined the Vitale brand from day one.

Over the years, the studio became more than just a place to paint — it was the site of legendary group art, music, and fashion shows in the late '90s and early 2000s. The mission was always to give exposure to local artists while learning and being inspired by them. Those events fostered St. Petersburg's emerging art scene and became the Vitale brand's own street art marketing machine.

The Crew Today

Today, Johnny leads a dedicated crew of talented artists under the Vitale Bros. banner. The team has created campaigns for the Tampa Bay Rays and L'Oreal's SalonCentric national locations, executed murals for the Tampa Bay Buccaneers, and transformed countless local businesses and private residences.

Commercial exterior painting, fine art reproductions, interior faux finishing, and custom signage are all part of the skill set. Every project is approached with the same passion and hustle that started it all — a napkin sketch and a dream.

Meet the Team

JV

Johnny Vitale

Founder / Lead Artist

Johnny Vitale started Vitale Bros. in 1992 with a hand-lettering kit, a stack of napkin sketches, and a habit of walking into St. Pete restaurants to pitch the owner cold. He still runs every project from the same scrappy, direct place. Under his lead, the studio has painted campaigns for the Tampa Bay Rays and L'Oreal's SalonCentric, murals for the Tampa Bay Buccaneers, and personal tributes like the Parc Center for Disabilities wall during SHINE 2022. Johnny's signature is the thing the studio is named for: paint that treats the wall like it matters.
