## [DESIGN-001] Hero headline + subhead options

Context: the current hero on `index.html` reads "Vitale Bros. / Making Walls Come Alive." with a subhead that starts "Tampa Bay's premier mural studio..." The critique from the design review was that the headline is generic, and the live site's "Bringing Art to the Community Since 1992" actually tells visitors who Vitale Bros. is in one line. These three options lead with different angles. Pick one, mix them, or redirect.

### Option A: Longevity first

Leads with the thing no competitor can match: 34 years of work in the same community.

- Kicker: St. Petersburg, FL, Est. 1992
- Headline: **Bringing Art to the Community Since 1992.**
- Subhead: Three decades of murals, live painting, and public art across St. Pete, Tampa, and beyond. A family studio that shows up, listens, and paints walls people want to stand in front of.
- Primary CTA: View Our Work
- Secondary CTA: Start a Project

**When this works best:** trust-building for first-time commercial clients and civic buyers who care about track record.

### Option B: St. Pete identity first

Leads with place. Positions Vitale Bros. as the studio that helped shape the St. Pete art scene, not just a vendor in it.

- Kicker: A St. Petersburg Original, Est. 1992
- Headline: **The Studio That Paints St. Pete.**
- Subhead: From SHINE Festival walls to backyard escapes, stadium murals to Main Street storefronts, Vitale Bros. has been shaping Tampa Bay's visual identity for over thirty years.
- Primary CTA: View Our Work
- Secondary CTA: Start a Project

**When this works best:** press, tourism, festival organizers, and anyone already bought into the St. Pete art scene. Most emotionally distinct of the three.

### Option C: Scope first

Leads with range. Answers the unspoken "can you do what I need?" question before a visitor has to scroll.

- Kicker: Muralists, Sign Painters, Live Artists, Est. 1992
- Headline: **Murals for Stadiums, Storefronts, and Everything in Between.**
- Subhead: Commercial, residential, civic, and live event work across Tampa Bay and the Southeast. If it has a wall, we can paint on it.
- Primary CTA: View Our Work
- Secondary CTA: Start a Project

**When this works best:** commercial buyers and brand managers comparing studios. Sets up the 4 service cards right below it without repeating them.

### Notes

- The "Est. 1992" kicker stays across all three, since it's the single most credible signal on the page.
- Subheads are all between 25 and 45 words. Safer for mobile than the current subhead.
- Let me know which one to ship, and I'll wire it into `index.html` and update the meta description to match.

### Johnny Vitale (Founder, Lead Artist)

**Option A, short (about 55 words):**

Johnny Vitale founded the studio in 1992 and still leads every project on the wall. He got his start hand lettering signs and storefront windows, then landed his first mural by walking into a restaurant with a sketch on a paper napkin. Three decades later, that same hustle drives every brief that comes through the door.

**Option B, medium (about 95 words):**

Johnny Vitale started Vitale Bros. in 1992 with a hand-lettering kit, a stack of napkin sketches, and a habit of walking into St. Pete restaurants to pitch the owner cold. He still runs every project from the same scrappy, direct place. Under his lead, the studio has painted campaigns for the Tampa Bay Rays and L'Oreal's SalonCentric, murals for the Tampa Bay Buccaneers, and personal tributes like the Parc Center for Disabilities wall during SHINE 2022. Johnny's signature is the thing the studio is named for: paint that treats the wall like it matters.

**Option C, quote-led (about 75 words):**

"This is not just for the general public, it's for them." That's Johnny on the 2022 Parc Center mural, and it's the line that explains why Vitale Bros. still exists 34 years after he founded it in 1992. Johnny got his start hand lettering signs and storefront windows, landed his first commission with a napkin sketch, and still runs every project from that same hustle. He's the studio's founder and lead artist.
