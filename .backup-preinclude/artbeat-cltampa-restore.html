For Pete's Sake:
Restoring Vandalized Murals

Street art is inherently vulnerable to the elements—and unfortunately, sometimes to vandalism. When a wave of tagging hit some of St. Petersburg's most beloved public murals, the Vitale Bros. knew it was time to step up and protect the creative fabric of the city we've helped build over the last three decades.

We've always viewed public art as a collaborative effort that belongs to the community. Witnessing pieces by our peers and friends defaced was disheartening, but rather than just complaining, we offered our services, materials, and time to professionally restore the damaged artworks at absolutely no cost.

"When vandals started to make their presence known we tried to battle that by saying, 'Look, we'll be the mural police or whatever,'" Johnny Vitale explained. "We're not after the vandals, we're after any of the damage that happens to any of these murals." Taking care of our fellow artists and keeping the Sunshine City shining is just what neighbors do.
